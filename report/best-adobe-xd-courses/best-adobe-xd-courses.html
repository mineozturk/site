What is Adobe XD?

Adobe XD (also known as Adobe Experience Design) is Adobe’s tool for prototyping user experience and creating designs with interactivity. Adobe XD features are used for creating wireframes, prototypes, and screen designs for digital products for web and mobile.

You can purchase Adobe XD as a standalone app for a monthly subscription of about $10, or all Adobe apps for about $55 (20+ Creative Cloud apps, including XD, Photoshop, Illustrator, and After Effects). Bear in mind there are other alternatives in the market if you are only interested in creating web and mobile designs, such as Figma and Sketch. If you want, check here our list for Best Figma Courses and Best Sketch Courses.

Course Ranking Methodology

I built this ranking following the now tried-and-tested methodology used in previous rankings (you can find them all here). It involves a three-step process:

  1. Research: I started by leveraging Class Central’s database with over 80K+ online courses and 170K reviews. Then, I made a preliminary selection of 80+ Adobe XD courses by rating, reviews, and bookmarks.
  2. Evaluate: I read through reviews on Class Central, Reddit, and course providers to understand what other learners thought about each course and combined it with my own experience as a learner.
  3. Select: Well-made courses were picked if they presented valuable and engaging content and they have to fit in a set of criteria and be ranked accordingly: release date, comprehensive curriculum, selling price, ratings and enrollments.

5. Introduction to Adobe XD (Domestika)

Learn with design specialist Ethan Parry, professor in Barcelona’s most reputed design universities. By the end of this course, you will be equipped with all necessary knowledge to take on a personal or professional UX design project.

Great course with excellent insight and information. Perfect for beginners and those looking to dive deeper into design. – questdotcom, learner

What You’ll Learn

In the first course, you will start by installing Adobe XD on your phone and computer, go through the layout of the software’s interface, and learn how to organize your workspace. Finish this course by trying out the design functions with Ethan.

Next, focus on designing an interface, learning the ins and outs of responsive design concepts to create the main screens of your application. Learn how to create components, create basic interactive prototypes, and finally how to use Adobe XD to showcase your designs.

Go a step further into prototyping with the third course, by creating dynamic effects and learning how to add carousels and voice to your prototype, along with micro-interactions with the use of component states. You’ll end this course by adding what you’ve learned to the designs you created in the previous course.

Discover how to share and collaborate on a UX project in the fourth course, seeing how to export artboards and elements by inviting someone to co-edit your design with you. See how to get feedback on your project so far, with the user testing mode.

In the final course, Ethan shares his tips and tricks to improve your design workflow using plugins, extensions, and UI kits. See where Ethan finds his plugins and apply various to your own app, to give a more dynamic design system to your final project.

8. Introducción a Adobe XD para aplicaciones móviles (Domestika)

In this series, which comprises 5 courses by Arturo Servín, web designer and Adobe Certified Instructor, you will learn how to design an app for iOS and Android like a professional. This course audio is in Spanish, with subtitles in English.

I like how Arturo explains everything straight to the point, by the end of the course you can do your own app design in XD with no problems, you have all the relevant info to get started. – cristinamaria, learner

What You’ll Learn

The first thing you will do is understand the workflow of a document in Adobe XD, know the different modules of its interface, how to use the properties window and the work tables, and then create your first document in this software to start structuring the design of your application.

You will learn to use the tools to sort, align and distribute items within the work tables. You will create simple ways to turn them into icons that you will integrate into your application and see how you can develop and integrate typographic styles.

Then you will create the application interface. You will see how to import images and work with symbols in your project. Then you will know the Grid tool to optimize processes, and Arturo will explain how to integrate your user interface for iOS and Android.

You will continue your learning, this time focused on something fundamental: the organization. You will discover the utility of working with smart guides and learn how to use the layer panel properly. Next, you will see how to work with folders and, also, how to fix items within the application. Then, you will see the advantages of working with an Adobe CC library.

To end this Adobe XD Domestika Basics, you will create an interactive prototype of your application. Arturo will explain the importance of the initial screen and, once you have built all the screens of the application, it will teach you how to link the sections, as well as how to edit and correct the links. You will see different preview options, to finally know the different ways to share your prototype and receive comments.
