What is Photography?

Even though it does not need an explanation, photography, in simple words, means taking photos using a camera. To quote photographylife,

Photography is the art of capturing light with a camera, usually via a digital sensor or film, to create an image. With the right camera equipment, you can even photograph wavelengths of light invisible to the human eye, including UV, infrared, and radio.

Adobe classifies photography into 28 different types and styles. Some of the common ones are:

  • Portrait photography
  • Wedding photography
  • Commercial photography
  • Travel photography
  • Event photography
  • Fashion photography
  • Sports photography
  • Wildlife photography
  • Architectural photography
  • Food photography

Why are Photography Skills Important?

According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for photographers was $18.73 in May 2021, whereas employment of photographers is projected to grow 17 percent from 2020 to 2030, much faster than the average for all occupations. More information on wage statistics can be found here.

The median annual wage for camera operators, television, video, and film was $49,230 in May 2021, and that for film and video editors was $62,680 in May 2021, whereas the overall employment of film and video editors and camera operators is projected to grow 29 percent from 2020 to 2030, much faster than the average for all occupations.(U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics).

According to IBISWorld, the market size of photography in the U.S. is $10.1 billion in 2022. AnythingResearch states that in 2020, the Federal Government of the United States spent a total of $15,889,472 on Photography. It has awarded 100 contracts to 49 companies, with an average value of $324,275 per company.

Moreover, a research by PR Newswire has shown that the global digital photography market, which is growing at a CAGR (compound annual growth rate) of 7.4% during the forecast period, is expected to reach $149.98 billion by 2026.

What is My Experience with Photography?

Ever since I was a child, my dad (a civil engineer) had encouraged me to learn photography. I’ve learned from him as he took photos during Durga Puja, vacations, family weddings, and even at home. I’ve used several DSLRs and lenses, and I’m fairly confident in my photography skills.

I have always been inspired by National Geographic. Their style is raw, stands out from the rest, and every picture tells a story. Someday, I’d love to see them work, if not work with them.

Besides that, I am a Guided Project Instructor and a Beta Tester at Coursera, having tested many courses before they’re officially launched. I have taken over 50 online courses in various subjects.

My experience as an online learner and teacher has given me some perspective on what to look for in an online course. I used my experience to evaluate each course in this list.

Course Ranking Methodology

I built this ranking following the now tried-and-tested methodology I used in previous rankings (you can find them all here). It involves a three-step process:

  1. Research: I started by leveraging Class Central’s database with 100K online courses and 200K+ reviews. Then, I made a preliminary selection of 1.1K+ Photography courses by rating, reviews, and bookmarks.
  2. Evaluate: I read through reviews on Class Central, Reddit, and course providers to understand what other learners thought about each course and combined it with my own experience as a learner.
  3. Select: Well-made courses were picked if they presented valuable and engaging content and they have to fit in a set of criteria and be ranked accordingly: comprehensive curriculum, release date, affordability, ratings and enrollments.

The end result is a unique selection of courses that combines a decade of Class Central data and my own experience as an online learner to try to get the best of both worlds. So far, I’ve spent more than 13 hours building this list, and I’ll continue to update it.

Course Ranking Statistics

My first pick for the best Photography online course is Master Photography | 26Hr Complete Guide Includes Workbook on Udemy.

Finally, you’ll learn an often overlooked camera setting and see light in a, well, different light, how to read a Histogram for perfect exposures every time, use Composition for better photos, and the first lens you should buy and why.

5. Fundamentals of Photography (CreativeLive)

My fifth pick for the best Photography online course is Fundamentals of Photography on CreativeLive.

Taught by seasoned photographer John Greengo, this course will help you master the technical basics of the camera and understand the kind of equipment you need. In this course, you’ll learn something more than just taking photos – to think about your ultimate goals and identify areas for growth.

What You’ll Learn

First, you’ll learn about photographic characteristics, camera types, viewing system, lens system, shutter system, shutter speed basics, shutter speed effects, camera and lens stabilization, camera settings, drive mode and buffer, and sensor sizes.

Next, you’ll learn about pixels, ISO, focal length, angle of view, fisheye lens, tilt and shift lens, subject zone, lens speed, aperture, depth of field (DOF), lens quality, light meter basics, histogram, dynamic range, exposure modes, and sunny 16 rule.

Then, you’ll take a look at exposure bracketing, exposure values, focusing basics, auto focus (AF), focus points, focus tracking, manual focus, digital focus assistance, shutter speeds depth of field (DOF), DOF preview and focusing screens, and lens sharpness.

You’ll also learn about camera movement, advanced techniques, auto focus calibration, focus stacking, camera accessories, lens accessories, lens adaptors and cleaning, macro, flash and lighting, tripods, cases, natural light such as direct sunlight, indirect sunlight, and mixed.

Next, you’ll explore in shooting sunrise and sunset light such as twilight, cloud and color pop, silhouette and starburst, and golden hour, light management, flash fundamentals, speedlights, built-in and add-on flash, off-camera flash, off-camera flash for portraits, and advanced flash techniques.

After that, you’ll learn about editing set-up, importing images, organizing your images, culling images, categories of development, adjusting exposure, removing distractions, cropping your images, composition basics, point of view, angle of view, subject placement, and framing your shot.

You’ll also take a look at foreground, background and scale, rule of odds, bad composition, multi-shot techniques, pixel shift, time lapse, selective cloning and noise reduction, human vision vs. the camera, visual perception, and visual drama.

Finally, you’ll end the course by learning about the elements of design, texture and negative space, black and white and color, the photographic process, working the shot, and what makes a great photograph.

How You’ll Learn

The course is broken down into 107 lessons. Each lesson involves about 5 to 30 minutes of work. Concepts are taught through a combination of videos and readings.
