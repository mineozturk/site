What is Graphic Design?

According to the American Institute of Graphic Arts (AIGA), graphic design may be defined as “the art and practice of planning and projecting ideas and experiences with visual and textual content.” One way to think of this is that, graphic design means taking graphical and textual elements and creating meaningful visuals out of them (mainly using technology).

Graphic designs usually have a purpose, such as commercial advertisements, but can also be used purely as a form of artistic expression. The main intention is to convey a message to the viewer in order to connect with them.

Why are Graphic Design Skills Important?

According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for graphic designers was $50,710 in May 2021.

Employment of graphic designers is projected to grow 3 percent from now to 2031, while, on average, about 24,800 graphic designing jobs in the U.S. are projected each year over the decade.

However, even though the need for digitally based graphic design is growing, a decrease in print media may limit opportunities for graphic designers who create print-based publications.

In a 2021 article, Forbes said that AI is making its way into the world of graphic design. Technology is bringing sophisticated design tools to us, replicating human human work and providing a more efficient outcome. However, as long as we make the correct use of technology, AI cannot replace human touch, and graphic designers will still be in the market.

Course Ranking Methodology

I built this ranking following the now tried-and-tested methodology I used in previous rankings (you can find them all here). It involves a three-step process:

  1. Research: I started by leveraging Class Central’s database with 100K online courses and 200K+ reviews. Then, I made a preliminary selection of 820+ Graphic Design courses by rating, reviews, and bookmarks.
  2. Evaluate: I read through reviews on Class Central, Reddit, and course providers to understand what other learners thought about each course and combined it with my own experience as a learner.
  3. Select: Well-made courses were picked if they presented valuable and engaging content and they have to fit in a set of criteria and be ranked accordingly: comprehensive curriculum, release date, affordability, ratings and enrollments.

The end result is a unique selection of courses that combines a decade of Class Central data and my own experience as an online learner to try to get the best of both worlds. So far, I’ve spent more than 17 hours building this list, and I’ll continue to update it.

Then, you’ll take a look at the books to read and education needed as a graphic designer, whether you need to go to university, qualifications and equipment needed to be a graphic designer, and programs needed as a graphic designer.

After that, you’ll learn about the graphic design portfolio, whether you should make a print or digital graphic design portfolio, the graphic design CV, career change to be a graphic designer, beginning as a graphic designer by applying your skills, and finding a graphic design job.

Finally, you’ll explore interview preparation to be a graphic designer, questions asked and to ask at a graphic design interview, interview tips to be a graphic designer, what the industry is like for a graphic designer, and how to further your graphic design career and the necessary education.

3. Graphic Design (California Institute of the Arts)

My third pick for the best Graphic Design online course is the free-to-audit Graphic Design, offered by California Institute of the Arts on Coursera.

This four-part specialization will teach you all that you need to know in order to create eye-catching designs: process, historical context, and communication through image-making and typography. These skills will prepare you to pursue formal studies in graphic design, so that you can take your graphic design skills further.

What You’ll Learn

Fundamentals of Graphic Design (4 weeks)

In this course you’ll learn the fundamental principles of graphic design like imagemaking, typography, composition, working with color and shape – foundational skills that form the base in all areas of graphic design.

Introduction to Typography (4 weeks)

In this course, you’ll study, name, and measure the characteristics of letterforms. You’ll take a look at the history of typefaces, discuss the best practices in setting type, and explore the expressive, meaning-making potential of type.

Introduction to Imagemaking (4 weeks)

In this course, you’ll experiment with a range of materials and techniques to make images for graphic design, expand your visual vocabulary, and learn how to make, manipulate and arrange images to create compositions.

Ideas from the History of Graphic Design (4 weeks)

In this course, you’ll explore ideas that helped formulate design practice from the 1850s through the 1960s to the ways that designers think today through social and cultural contexts, technologies, aesthetics, and politics of their eras.

Brand New Brand (6 weeks)

This course takes you through the entire design process, from ideation to creation to presentation. Through the guide, you will be creating and giving a visual identity to an imaginary start-up company, and applying that visual identity to a number of forms.

How You’ll Learn

This specialization is broken down into 5 courses, and each course is broken down into 4 modules , except the last one which is 6 weeks. Each module involves about 30 minutes to 1 hour of work. Concepts are taught through a combination of videos, readings and quizzes.

5. Graphic Design (University of Colorado Boulder)

My fifth pick for the best Graphic Design online course is the free-to-audit Graphic Design, offered by University of Colorado Boulder on Coursera.

This a a practical course that not only teaches you the theory and basics of graphic design, but actually encourages you to apply those concepts and create your own designs in basic presentation software such as Microsoft PowerPoint, Google Slides, or Apple Keynote, or even more sophisticated tools, such as Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator, and InDesign.

What You’ll Learn

In week 1, you’ll be introduced to the importance of recognizing good design. You’ll learn to establish a vocabulary of successful design and find inspiration for our own projects.

In week 2, you’ll learn the importance of working with a grid, negative space, appropriate margins, and the palette of elements. You’ll also cover type, color, images, and graphic elements such as borders, bars, and backgrounds.

In week 3, you’ll use the palette items covered in week 2, apply contrast, repetition, sublimation and tension, and cover the strategy of composition.

In week 4, you’ll understand what it means to be confident, yet open to criticism. You’ll explore the importance of revision, as well as the need to enlist feedback and keep distance in assessing our own work.

How You’ll Learn

The course is broken down into 4 modules. Each module involves about 4 to 10 hours of work. Concepts are taught through a combination of videos, readings and quizzes.

10. Introduction to Graphic Design (LinkedIn Learning)

My tenth pick for the best Graphic Design online course is the free course, Introduction to Graphic Design on LinkedIn Learning.

This course covers both the theory and basic concepts of graphic design as well as software skills such as Photoshop, Illustrator, and InDesign. Master all the topics to get started as a graphic designer. This course also comes with a free certificate from LinkedIn Learning!

What You’ll Learn

In the first module, you’ll get an overview of the creative process. You’ll take a look at the creative brief, research, ideation, digital ideation, and production.

In the second module, you’ll take a look at the basics of layout and composition, the design space, principles of layout, grids, and layout analysis.

In the third module, you’ll explore the language of type, type terminology, type anatomy, basic type classifications, ligatures, type measurement, text elements like character and paragraph, selecting type, basic typography guidelines, and type crimes.

In the fourth module, you’ll learn about the color components, monochrome and temperature, color harmonies, RGB and CMYK, process and spot colors, color associations, and color blindness.

In the fifth module, you’ll get a Photoshop quick start tour. You’ll learn about saving a PSD file from Photoshop, making basic selections in Photoshop, placing and transforming other assets, and making and refining color selections.

You’ll also learn about selecting and refining hair strands, selectively changing colors, using content-aware scale, cropping an image, and making a black-and-white adjustment.

In the sixth module, you’ll get started with Illustrator. You’ll import a rough sketch, draw the logo, create a color theme, create point type in Illustrator, use the Touch Type tool, and Creative Cloud libraries.

In the eighth module, you’ll learn how to use InDesign. You’ll create a new document, working with master pages, import graphics into InDesign, do roundtrip editing with Photoshop, and adjust the fit of graphics.
